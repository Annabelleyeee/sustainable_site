Goals and Initiatives

The overacrching goal for sustainbility at Rangi Ruru is to: Create greater awareness and participation in Rangi’s sustainability and service initiatives through education, communication and collaboration within the school and wider community through service, Eco-Action and community collaborations

Current Goals

Biodigester

We are seeking funding and the opportunity to work alongside business to design and install a biodigestor onsite. This will allow Rangi to convert green waste to biogas (to replace non-renewable natural gas currently used for cooking in the Boarding House and heating in Te Koroha and the pool). This investment enables substantial economic savings of which 60% will be reinvested into sustainability projects once establishment costs are covered after twenty six months.

Objectives

  • Establish the use of 100% renewable energy at Rangi and provide a high degree of energy independence for all parts of the school’s operation
  • Take advantage of the energy sources already present onsite (and potentially within the wider community by using green waste from neighbours to create biogas)
  • Reduce the waste discharged by the school and reduce waste disposal cost
  • Significantly lower the operating costs of the school, pool and boarding facility

Current Initiatives

Food and Drink

  • Got rid of vending machine, highly processed food and drinks
  • Increased onsite production of healthy food
  • Changed to biodegradable cutlery, plates and bowls
  • Switched to using free range eggs only
  • Established Meat-Free Mondays

Energy and Water

  • Solar energy on Gibson Centre roof
  • Planning to establish a bio-digester onsite to use all green waste to create renewable energy

Travel and Traffic

  • Walk and Wheel, Waste-less Wednesdays part of our Healthy Habits
  • Weekly Green Tips (Intouch & assembly) from Sustainability Council to create greater community awareness
  • Established bus route from Burnham/Rolleston area
  • Planning for bike repair course in Environment Club and cycle awareness.

Purchasing and waste

  • Established Rangi Reuse Depot diverting thousands of items of waste
  • Set up source separation of waste and education/awareness
  • Started recycling all yellow bin products not just paper
  • Collections of GOOS paper for reuse established and maintained
  • Reduced waste to landfill by 62% and halved skip collections
  • Year 7/8 Challenge Based Learning finding solutions to waste
  • Stopped the use of individually packaged single use butter
  • All year 9 classes to visit CCC facilities in Social Studies/Science
  • Established composting and worm farm onsite

Buildings and Grounds

  • Environmentally friendly building design (natural lighting, sensor lights, thermal mass, replaceable floor tiles, water-based paints
  • Drought resistance planting of native trees and shrubs

Inclusion and Participation

  • Finalist in the 2018 NZI Sustainable Business Network Awards Hardwired for Social Good category, and Millennial son a Mission 2019, KNZB Sustainable School, and Young Legends Award 2019. Students finalists or winners in Girls Boss Sustainability, Speaking 4 the Planet and KNZB Young Reporters for the Environment
  • International collaborations and learning exchanges
  • Education for Sustainability NCEA standards
  • Sustainability hui hosted by Rangi
  • Whānau invited to participate on sustainability projects
  • Actively involved in Eco-Action project and native nursery onsite

Wellbeing

  • Gratitude diary
  • Established daily Healthy Habits
  • Positive Psychology - Feeling good by doing good
  • Spending time in our onsite nursery working together

Local Connections

  • Joined Enviroschools network with kaupapa to guide learning across the curriculum and foster relationships with community groups

  • Supporting a long-term regeneration project to re-establish native fauna and flora into Drayton Reserve including native ruru (owl) and weta and established collaborative Eco-Action nursery onsite

  • Actively involved in Mother of all Clean Up’s and also run our own river clean ups
  • All students are involved in Student Volunteer Army Service Awards

  • >Have protected an area of rainforest the size of our school grounds
